# Rubik’s Cube Step Image Prompts

Use these prompts to generate supplemental images for `index.html`.

## Shared style for every image

Create a clean kid-friendly instructional illustration on a white background. Use a classic 3x3 Rubik’s Cube with flat bright sticker colors: white, yellow, red, orange, blue, and green. Use simple vector-like shapes, thick friendly outlines, soft shadow, and clear arrows. Avoid photorealism, clutter, tiny text, extra cubes, distorted sticker counts, or impossible cube geometry. The image should look like a printable guide for a young beginner.

---

## 01.webp — Step 1: Make a white daisy

Illustrate a 3x3 Rubik’s Cube viewed mostly from the top. The top face has a yellow center square. The four edge squares around the yellow center are white, forming a flower/daisy shape. The four top-face corner squares can be neutral gray or mixed colors so the white edge “petals” stand out. Add a small friendly arrow or highlight around the four white edge petals. Intent: show that the learner should hold yellow on top and place four white edge pieces around the yellow center.

## 02.webp — Step 2: Turn the daisy into a white cross

Create a simple before-and-after instructional illustration. On the left, show the top yellow center with four white edge petals around it. Add an arrow pointing to the right. On the right, show the cube tilted so the bottom/white face is visible, with a white cross on the white face. Also show the side colors of the cross edges matched with their center colors on the side faces. Intent: show that each daisy petal gets matched to its side center, then turned down to make the white cross.

## 03.webp — Step 3: Put in the white corners

Show a cube with the white cross already on the bottom. Highlight the front-right-bottom corner slot. Above that slot on the top layer, show a white corner piece ready to go home, with its side colors matching the front and right center colors. Add a curved arrow from the top corner down into the front-right-bottom slot. Optionally include the move helper as small readable text: “R U R’ U’”. Intent: show placing one white corner above its correct home and dropping it into the bottom layer.

## 04.webp — Step 4: Fill the middle floor

Show a cube with the white bottom layer solved. Highlight the middle layer around the cube as the “middle floor.” On the top layer, show an edge piece with no yellow sticker matched to the front center color. Include two small side-by-side mini diagrams: one arrow sending the edge to the right, and one arrow sending an edge to the left. Use clear left/right arrows, but keep the image simple. Intent: show how a non-yellow top edge moves into the middle layer to the left or right.

## 05.webp — Step 5: Make a yellow cross

Show four small top-face diagrams in a row with arrows between them: yellow dot, yellow little L, yellow line, yellow cross. The top face should be the yellow face; only the yellow edge stickers matter. For the line case, show the line going left-right. For the L case, show the yellow edge stickers pointing to the back and left. Optionally include the move spell as small readable text: “F R U R’ U’ F’”. Intent: show the yellow-top pattern changing until it becomes a yellow plus sign.

## 06.webp — Step 6: Line up the yellow cross side colors

Show a cube with a yellow cross on top. On the side faces, highlight the yellow-cross edge pieces’ side stickers matching their center colors. Show two adjacent matched edges at the back and right with check marks. Add a gentle circular arrow around the top layer to show turning only the top to check matches. Intent: show that the yellow cross exists, and now the side colors of those yellow edges must line up with their centers.

## 07.webp — Step 7: Put yellow corners in their homes

Show a cube with the yellow cross solved on top. Highlight the four top-layer corner pieces. Use one corner at the front-right-top with a small house/check mark to show it is in its correct home, even if twisted. Add curved arrows around the other top corners to show they move around until each corner is in its correct home. Intent: show that yellow corners need to be moved to the correct locations before twisting them yellow-side-up.

## 08.webp — Step 8: Twist yellow corners to finish

Show the final yellow-corner twisting step. The cube should have all pieces solved except one highlighted top front-right corner that needs twisting so yellow faces upward. Add a circular twist arrow on that corner and a separate top-layer arrow labeled visually as “turn top only” if text is acceptable. Also show a small finished cube with all faces solved as the end result. Intent: show twisting each yellow corner in place while not rotating the whole cube, finishing with a solved cube.
